Tarator

Description

Tarator is a traditional cold soup originating from the Balkan region, popular in countries like Bulgaria, Greece, Macedonia, and Turkey. This refreshing dish is ideal for warm weather, combining yogurt, cucumbers, garlic, and herbs such as dill and mint. Some variations include walnuts for added texture. The soup is seasoned with salt and sometimes vinegar or lemon juice, and it's served chilled. Known for its cooling and hydrating properties, tarator offers a delightful blend of tangy yogurt, crisp cucumbers, and aromatic herbs, making it a nutritious and popular choice in hot climates.

Ingredients

  • Yogurt
  • Cucumbers
  • Garlic
  • Dill (herb)
  • Mint (herb)
  • Parsley (herb)
  • Walnuts (optional, for added texture)
  • Salt
  • Vinegar or Lemon juice (for seasoning)
  • Olive oil (for garnish, optional)

Steps

  1. Combine yogurt and water to achieve a soupy consistency.
  2. Add chopped cucumbers to the yogurt mixture.
  3. Minced garlic and finely chopped herbs (dill, mint, and parsley) are added to the mixture.
  4. Season the soup with salt and, optionally, vinegar or lemon juice for added flavor.
  5. Stir the soup thoroughly to ensure an even distribution of flavors.
  6. Optionally, include walnuts for added texture.
  7. Serve the Tarator chilled.
  8. Garnish with additional herbs or a drizzle of olive oil if desired.
